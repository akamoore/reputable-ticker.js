Opening

1-2 minutes

Frame the "why" — the team has been asking for clarity on how participants move through our ecosystem. This presentation answers that question end-to-end.

  • We've spent the last week mapping out exactly how participants move through our ecosystem, from first ad click to long-term retention.
  • The core idea: treat participants like customers. This is an end-to-end lifecycle, just like any e-commerce brand manages their customer journey.
  • Today we're walking through where things stand, what's working, what we need, and what we're asking the product team to help us build.
  • We're not asking for everything at once. We have a phased plan with clear priorities.

Don't spend long here. Get to the lifecycle visual quickly — that's what grabs attention.

1 Lead Capture

OneLinks & UTM Tracking

  • We now have OneLinks and UTM parameters set up for every channel starting in January.
  • Problem: they weren't being pulled into the dashboard. Manpreet is fixing that.
  • Once that's in place, we can finally answer "where are our participants actually coming from?"
2 App Install

The Biggest Leak in the Funnel

  • 75% of people who click our ads never download the app. That's the single biggest leak in our funnel.
  • We're working with Matt on direct app install flows to close that gap.
  • AppsFlyer is our source of truth for install attribution, but the data isn't matching what's in our dashboard yet.
3 Browse / Explore

The Silent Drop-Off

  • People who download the app but never join a study — we have no way to reach them right now.
  • We need push notifications and in-app messaging to nudge these people toward studies.
  • This is a Phase 2 item, but important to flag for the team.
4 Eligibility Survey KEY MOMENT

Where We Lost 100+ People Before We Knew

  • This is the big one. Tell the story:
  • We had no visibility into where people were dropping off by question. By the time we discovered there was a problem, over 100 people had already gone through.
  • The ask is simple: real-time drop-off monitoring by question so we can catch problems within the first 10-20 people, not after 100.
  • Example: the GLP-1/metformin question in the HOP study may be unnecessarily disqualifying people.

This stage gets the most reaction. Pause here and let it sink in.

5 Onboarding

"Joined" Is Not "Onboarded"

  • Key distinction: "joined" is not the same as "fully onboarded."
  • ProLivaMax is the perfect example: people join but then don't complete their labs. We have no visibility into who's stuck in that manual queue.
  • For any study with manual steps (labs, wearable setup, scheduling calls), we need a manual queue status showing pending vs. completed.
  • Right now, Nisha tells me we need more people, but can't give me an exact number. That has to change.
6 Active in Study

Stop Recruiting Active Participants

  • Once someone is active, they need to be automatically excluded from all recruitment campaigns.
  • I've accidentally reached out to people already in studies because I had no visibility. That's not a good experience for participants.
  • Compliance monitoring and at-risk flagging are Phase 2, but the exclusion piece is essential now.
7 Study Completion

Automate the Transition

  • When someone finishes a study, that should automatically trigger the next part of their journey.
  • Exit survey → washout period starts → drip campaign until ready for next study.
  • Right now, none of this is automated. It's all manual tracking.
8 Washout Period

The Invisible Queue

  • There's no automated way to know who's in washout, who's done, and who's available.
  • During washout, participants could be doing public studies to stay engaged — but we're not nudging them.
  • When washout ends, they should get a notification: "You're eligible for new studies." That doesn't exist today.
9 Re-engagement

Smart Segmentation, Not Blasts

  • This is the vision. Once we can segment properly, we stop sending one blast to everyone.
  • Pankaj's experience at Cycleic Water is the model: 12 emails a month, but each person only received 2-3 because of smart segmentation.
  • Churn detection: if someone hasn't logged in for 3+ months, we should know and have a re-engagement campaign ready.
  • Incentives like bonus heartbeats can pull dormant users back in.

Point out the loop — participants cycle through stages 3-9 multiple times. That's what makes this a lifecycle, not a funnel.

Segmentation Rules

2-3 minutes

Keep this short. These filters don't exist today, and that's why everyone gets the same email.

  • Right now, every participant gets the same weekly newsletter blast regardless of where they are in their journey.
  • We can't distinguish between someone active in a study, someone in washout, and someone who churned 6 months ago.
  • These 8 segmentation rules are what unlock targeted, relevant communication.
  • This is also what prevents embarrassing mistakes — like reaching out to someone already in a study.
Active in study (exclude from recruitment)
In washout period (engage with public studies)
Completed washout (eligible for new studies)
Never joined a study (nurture campaign)
Churned (no login 3+ months)
Lead source / acquisition channel
Study eligibility criteria match
Engagement level (active vs. dormant)

The ask: We need these as query-able filters in the dashboard. Even if it's manual pulls at first, we need the ability to generate these lists.

Implementation Phases

3-5 minutes

Set expectations clearly — we are NOT asking for everything at once.

Phase 1: Minimum Viable

Now → 4 weeks
  • Data cleanup is urgent. Test and dummy accounts are inflating our numbers and hurting email deliverability. We had 2,000 bounces on our last send because of this.
  • Naming convention for test accounts going forward so they're auto-excluded from filters.
  • Basic segmentation queries — even if I'm pulling lists manually, I just need the ability to say "give me everyone not in an active study and not in washout."
  • Eligibility drop-off by question — critical for every study launch going forward.
  • Dashboard fixes: accurate spend numbers, source attribution, 30-day views.

Phase 2: Full System

4 wks → 3 months
  • This is where we get to automation: one-click email campaigns, in-app notifications, washout tracking, churn detection.
  • The infrastructure from Phase 1 makes Phase 2 possible.
  • Phase 1 alone will dramatically improve what we can do. Phase 2 is the long-term vision.

Emphasize this framing: "We're defining what we need. We're asking the product team to tell us how to implement it." You're not telling them how to build it.
