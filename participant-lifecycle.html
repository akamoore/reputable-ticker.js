The 9 Stages

1 Lead Capture

Ads, Newsletter, Referral, Organic, Self-Serve

Where participants first enter our ecosystem

Channels

  • Meta Ads (Facebook & Instagram)
  • Google Ads / Search
  • Newsletter signup forms
  • Referral links from existing participants
  • Organic search / blog content
  • Self-serve study landing pages

Systems

  • OneLinks with UTM parameters (since Jan 2026)
  • AppsFlyer for install attribution
  • Meta Ads Manager for spend data
UTM tracking recently added Attribution not in dashboard yet
2 App Install

The Biggest Leak in the Funnel

75% of people who click our ads never download the app

Pain Points

  • 75% drop-off from ad click to install
  • AppsFlyer data doesn't match dashboard
  • No direct app install flow from ads

Target State

  • Direct app install flows from ads
  • Unified install attribution (single source of truth)
  • Install-to-signup tracking
Biggest funnel leak Working with Matt on fix
3 Browse / Explore

The Silent Drop-Off

Installed the app but never joined a study

Pain Points

  • No way to reach these users today
  • No push notifications or in-app nudges
  • Can't track browse-only users

Target State

  • Push notifications to nudge toward studies
  • In-app messaging and recommendations
  • Browse-to-join conversion tracking
No engagement tools Phase 2 priority
4 Eligibility Survey CRITICAL

Where We Lost 100+ People Before We Knew

No visibility into question-level drop-off until it was too late

Pain Points

  • 100+ people went through broken flow before discovery
  • No question-level drop-off visibility
  • GLP-1/metformin question may be unnecessarily disqualifying
  • Problems only caught after massive damage

Target State

  • Real-time drop-off monitoring by question
  • Catch problems in first 10-20 people
  • Alert system for abnormal disqualification rates
  • Question-level analytics for every study launch
No question-level data Phase 1 priority
5 Onboarding

"Joined" Is Not "Onboarded"

Participants join but then get stuck in manual queues

Pain Points

  • No visibility into who's stuck in manual queue
  • Can't get exact headcount from study leads
  • Manual steps: labs, wearable setup, scheduling calls

Target State

  • Manual queue status: pending vs. completed
  • Real-time onboarding progress per participant
  • Exact numbers always available
Fully manual tracking No queue visibility
6 Active in Study

Stop Recruiting Active Participants

Active participants must be excluded from all recruitment

Pain Points

  • No auto-exclusion from campaigns
  • Accidentally recruiting active participants
  • No compliance monitoring or at-risk flagging

Target State

  • Auto-exclude from all recruitment campaigns
  • Real-time active status in dashboard
  • Compliance monitoring (Phase 2)
No auto-exclusion Phase 1 essential
7 Study Completion

Automate the Transition

Completion should trigger the next phase of their journey

Pain Points

  • No automated post-completion workflow
  • Manual tracking of who finished and when
  • No exit survey or transition triggers

Target State

  • Auto-trigger: exit survey → washout starts → drip campaign
  • Automated transition to next lifecycle stage
  • Completion analytics per study
All manual tracking Phase 2
8 Washout Period

The Invisible Queue

No automated way to know who's in washout or when it ends

Pain Points

  • No automated tracking of washout periods
  • Can't tell who's done vs. still waiting
  • No engagement during washout window

Target State

  • Auto-track washout start/end dates
  • Nudge toward public studies during washout
  • Notification when eligible: "You're ready for new studies"
Completely invisible Phase 2
9 Re-engagement

Smart Segmentation, Not Blasts

Targeted communication based on where each person is in their journey

Pain Points

  • Everyone gets the same weekly blast
  • No churn detection (3+ month inactivity)
  • No segmentation filters available

Target State

  • Smart segmentation: 12 emails/mo, each person sees 2-3
  • Churn detection and re-engagement campaigns
  • Incentives (bonus heartbeats) to pull dormant users back
One-size-fits-all Phase 2 vision

Participants cycle through stages 3–9 multiple times. Each pass through the loop is an opportunity to improve their experience and our retention.
