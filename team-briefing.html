Executive Summary

TL;DR

Over the past week, we mapped out the complete participant lifecycle — from first ad click to long-term retention. The core insight: we need to treat participants like customers with an end-to-end journey, just like any e-commerce brand manages their customer lifecycle.

While doing this, we cross-referenced our dashboard against actual Meta Ads Manager exports and discovered a $4,200 gap in reported spend. ProleevaMax was undercounted by $1,561 (missing campaign) and Berkeley Life ($2,632) was missing entirely. These corrections have been applied to our internal Command Center.

The good news: our ecosystem has grown from ~100 to 800+ participants, our cost per qualified lead for standard studies is $15-20, and self-serve studies bring people in at zero acquisition cost. The challenge is that nearly everything in the participant journey is manual and disconnected. We've outlined a phased plan to fix that — starting with what we can do in the next 4 weeks.

We're not asking for everything at once. We have a phased plan with clear priorities. We're defining what we need — and asking the product team to tell us how to implement it.

5 Onboarding

"Joined" Is Not "Onboarded"

  • Key distinction: "joined" is not the same as "fully onboarded."
  • ProleevaMax is a clear example: people join but then don't complete their labs. There's no visibility into who's stuck in the manual queue.
  • For any study with manual steps (labs, wearable setup, scheduling calls), we need a manual queue status showing pending vs. completed.
  • Currently, the clinical team reports needing more people but can't give exact numbers. That has to change.
6 Active in Study

Stop Recruiting Active Participants

  • Once someone is active, they need to be automatically excluded from all recruitment campaigns.
  • Without visibility, recruitment has accidentally reached out to people already in studies. That's not a good participant experience.
  • Compliance monitoring and at-risk flagging are Phase 2, but the exclusion piece is essential now.
7 Study Completion

Automate the Transition

  • When someone finishes a study, that should automatically trigger the next part of their journey.
  • Exit survey → washout period starts → drip campaign until ready for next study.
  • Right now, none of this is automated. It's all manual tracking.
8 Washout Period

The Invisible Queue

  • There's no automated way to know who's in washout, who's done, and who's available.
  • During washout, participants could be doing public studies to stay engaged — but we're not nudging them.
  • When washout ends, they should get a notification: "You're eligible for new studies." That doesn't exist today.
9 Re-engagement

Smart Segmentation, Not Blasts

  • The vision: once we can segment properly, we stop sending one blast to everyone.
  • Pankaj's experience at Cycleic Water is the model: 12 emails a month, but each person only received 2-3 because of smart segmentation.
  • Churn detection: if someone hasn't logged in for 3+ months, we should know and have a re-engagement campaign ready.
  • Incentives like bonus heartbeats can pull dormant users back in.

Segmentation Rules

None of these filters exist today. Every participant gets the same weekly newsletter blast regardless of where they are in their journey. This is the single most impactful capability we need.

Why This Matters

With 800+ participants spread across multiple studies at different stages, blanket communication creates real problems. We've accidentally recruited people already in active studies, sent irrelevant emails to churned users, and missed opportunities to re-engage people who just completed washout. The Cycleic Water model (Pankaj's experience) shows how this should work: 12 emails a month going out, but each person only receives the 2-3 that are relevant to them.

The ask: We need these 8 rules as query-able filters in the dashboard. Even manual list pulls at first would be a huge step forward. The goal is to go from "one blast to everyone" to "the right message to the right person at the right time."

Active in study — Exclude from all recruitment campaigns. Currently no way to auto-filter these out, leading to duplicate outreach and poor participant experience.
In washout period — Engage with public studies and low-touch content. These participants are committed but can't start a new sponsored study yet. Keep them warm.
Completed washout — Eligible for new sponsored studies. These are the most valuable recruitment targets — already proven, already onboarded, zero acquisition cost.
Never joined a study — Nurture campaign needed. These people installed the app but haven't committed. They need education about what studies involve and what they get out of it.
Churned (3+ months inactive) — Re-engagement campaign with incentives like bonus heartbeats. If they don't respond, they should be cleaned from active email lists to protect deliverability.
Lead source / channel — Know whether a participant came from Meta ads, organic search, newsletter, or referral. Critical for optimizing where we spend ad dollars.
Eligibility criteria match — Filter by demographics, health conditions, and study-specific requirements. Enables targeted recruitment for studies like HOP, ProleevaMax, or any future study with specific criteria.
Engagement level — Distinguish between highly active participants (daily logins, completing tasks) vs. dormant ones. Tailor communication frequency and type accordingly.

Real-world example: Right now, our weekly newsletter goes to ~1,900 people with 130+ bounces per send. With these 8 segments, each email only goes to the relevant subset. Result: fewer bounces, higher engagement, better participant experience, and no accidental recruitment of active participants.

Implementation Phases

We are NOT asking for everything at once. We're defining what we need and asking the product team to tell us how to implement it. Phase 1 alone will dramatically improve what we can do.

Phase 1: Minimum Viable

Now → 4 weeks
  • Data cleanup (URGENT) — Test and dummy accounts are inflating our numbers and hurting email deliverability. We had 2,000 bounced emails on our last newsletter send because of dirty data. Need: remove test accounts, establish a naming convention (e.g. test_*) so they're auto-excluded from all filters going forward.
  • Basic segmentation queries — Even manual list pulls would be a huge step forward. Minimum: ability to query "give me everyone not in an active study and not in washout." This alone prevents duplicate recruitment and enables targeted outreach.
  • Eligibility drop-off by question — Critical for every study launch. We need to see where people are dropping off in the eligibility survey, by question, so we can catch issues within the first 10-20 people. We lost 100+ people in HOP before we knew there was a problem.
  • Dashboard spend accuracy — Fix the $4,200 gap. Ensure all campaigns are mapped (ProleevaMax had 5 campaigns but only 4 were tracked). Add Berkeley Life. Implement 30-day vs. all-time views. Manpreet is working on this.
  • Source attribution — OneLinks and UTMs are set up for every channel since January, but they're not being pulled into the dashboard yet. This is the foundation for knowing where participants come from.
  • Manual queue visibility — For studies with manual steps (ProleevaMax labs, wearable setup), we need a simple status: pending vs. completed. Right now, the clinical team can't give exact numbers on who's stuck.

Phase 2: Full System

4 wks → 3 months
  • Automated email campaigns — One-click segmented sends from the dashboard. Trigger-based drip campaigns for each lifecycle stage (welcome series, washout engagement, re-enrollment prompts).
  • Push notifications & in-app messaging — Reach the "silent drop-off" people who installed the app but never joined a study. Nudge them toward studies they're eligible for.
  • Washout period tracking — Auto-track when washout starts, when it ends, and notify participants when they're eligible for new studies. Today this is completely manual with no visibility.
  • Churn detection & re-engagement — Flag participants with no login for 3+ months. Auto-trigger re-engagement campaigns with incentives like bonus heartbeats.
  • Compliance monitoring — At-risk flagging for active study participants who stop completing tasks. Alert the clinical team before it becomes a dropout.
  • Auto-exclusion rules — Once someone joins a study, they're automatically removed from all recruitment campaigns for other studies. No more accidental double-recruitment.
  • Study completion transitions — When a participant finishes: auto-trigger exit survey → start washout timer → begin drip campaign → notify when eligible for next study.

Key point: Phase 1 infrastructure makes Phase 2 possible. The data cleanup, segmentation queries, and dashboard fixes are the foundation everything else builds on. We need Phase 1 right to unlock the full automation vision in Phase 2.

Integration Points

Almost everything is manual, disconnected, and living in different systems. The goal is one unified system where data flows in automatically and we have visibility without asking five different people for status updates.

Current State

Today, participant data lives across Meta Ads Manager, AppsFlyer, the app dashboard, email platform, individual spreadsheets, and clinical team notes. There's no automated data flow between these systems. Getting a simple answer like "how many ProleevaMax participants completed their labs this week?" requires asking Nisha directly, because that data doesn't surface anywhere in the dashboard.

The $4,200 spend gap we discovered is a direct result of this fragmentation — campaigns in Meta Ads weren't properly mapped to studies in the dashboard, and nobody caught it because the verification process is manual.

Meta Ads → Dashboard

Today Manually downloading lead data from Ads Manager. Campaign-to-study mapping is incomplete (caused the $4,200 gap). No real-time spend visibility.
Target Auto-feed spend and lead data into dashboard with UTM attribution. All campaigns mapped to studies. Real-time cost-per-lead by study.

Email System

Today One blast to ~1,900 people with 130+ bounces per send. No segmentation. Can't target by study status, lifecycle stage, or engagement level.
Target Segmented sends from dashboard (feature already started). Trigger-based drip campaigns. Automatic suppression of active study participants. Clean email list = fewer bounces.

AppsFlyer (Install Attribution)

Today Install data doesn't match dashboard. Can't confidently say which channel drives installs. 75% of ad clickers never install — but we can't track exactly where they drop off.
Target Unified install attribution — single source of truth. Tie ad click → install → study join into one cohesive journey. Required for accurate cost-per-install metrics.

Eligibility Surveys

Today No question-level drop-off visibility. Can't see which questions cause disqualifications. The GLP-1/metformin question in HOP may be unnecessarily filtering people out — we don't know.
Target Real-time monitoring: drop-off rate by question, disqualification reason breakdown, average time to complete. Catch problems in the first 10-20 people, not after 100+.

Participant Status

Today Need to ask 5 different people for status updates. No unified view of who's in which stage. "Joined" and "onboarded" are conflated — ProleevaMax shows 152 joins but only 35 onboarded because labs are the bottleneck.
Target One dashboard with real-time lifecycle status per participant. Clear distinction between joined/onboarding/active/completed/washout. Manual queue tracking for lab-dependent studies.

Washout Tracking

Today No automated tracking whatsoever. Can't tell who's in washout, how long they've been there, or when they'll be available. Participants in washout could be doing public studies to stay engaged — but we're not nudging them.
Target Auto-track washout start/end dates. Engage participants with public studies during washout. Auto-notify when eligible for new sponsored studies. These are our highest-value re-enrollment targets.

Bottom line: Every integration gap costs us either money (inaccurate spend data), participants (silent drop-offs), or time (manual status checks). Fixing even the Phase 1 integrations — Meta Ads mapping, email segmentation, eligibility monitoring — removes the biggest pain points immediately.
