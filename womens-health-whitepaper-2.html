Women's Health
The Research Gap
Assuming male findings predict female outcomes is a fundamentally flawed approach to science.
For much of modern medical history, clinical research predominantly focused on male physiology — leaving critical gaps in how we understand disease, treatment, and prevention in women.
⚠️
The result? Diseases manifest differently in women — and we're only beginning to understand how.
Symptoms get missed. Diagnoses get delayed. Treatments built on male data don't account for hormonal, immunological, and metabolic differences.
"The road to balanced healthcare requires a comprehensive understanding of the female exposome."
— Dr. Mackenzie De Jesus
